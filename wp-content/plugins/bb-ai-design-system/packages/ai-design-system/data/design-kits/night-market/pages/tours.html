Questions

Things people
actually ask.

How much will I eat? Do I need to skip dinner?

Yes. Skip dinner. The tour covers eight full tastings and you will be full by stop five. Most people eat a very light lunch on tour days. Don't try to be disciplined about portion sizes at each stall — that defeats the purpose. You can always say no to a dish, but you'll regret it at least twice.

What's the cancellation policy?

Full refund up to 48 hours before departure. Within 48 hours the card is charged. Within 24 hours, no refund — the stall vendors have been told to expect us and some of them pre-prepare quantities. If you're sick, email us. We'll put you on the waitlist for the next available tour at no extra cost.

I have a severe allergy. Can I still join?

It depends on the allergy and the market. Shellfish and sesame are difficult in most markets — they appear in stalls that cook everything in the same woks. Tree nut allergies are generally manageable. Gluten is tour-dependent. Email us before booking with your specific allergy and we'll tell you honestly whether it's workable or whether a private booking with a custom route is the safer option.

How much walking is involved?

Between 3.5 and 5 kilometres depending on the tour, at a relaxed pace with stops. You'll mostly stand while eating. Good walking shoes are required — night markets are paved but surfaces vary and you'll be on your feet for three to four hours. Wheelchair accessibility varies by market; email us and we'll assess.

Do you run tours when it rains?

Yes. Night markets run in the rain; the vendors have tarps and so do we. Light rain is actually better — fewer tourists, shorter queues, more atmospheric. We only cancel for typhoon warnings or government closures. Bring a compact umbrella. Don't wear anything you'd be upset about getting splashed.
