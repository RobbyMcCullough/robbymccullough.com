## Concept

The Timberline is a boutique mountain lodge in South Lake Tahoe, CA, near the Nevada Stateline. The lodge caters primarily to skiers and snowboarders, offering a daily shuttle service to Heavenly Mountain, Sierra-at-Tahoe, and Kirkwood. The brand sits between a cozy mountain cabin and an elevated boutique hotel — warm, welcoming, and slightly premium without feeling pretentious. The surrounding environment is key: pine forest, snow, casino lights in the distance, and three world-class mountains nearby. Guests choose The Timberline for the access (5 minutes from Heavenly), the community (a place to actually hang out après-ski), and the detail (good food, gear storage, a real hot tub).

The visual identity should feel like a high-quality editorial outdoor brand — think Outside Magazine meets a small independent boutique hotel that takes its design seriously. NOT dark moody wilderness. NOT corporate ski resort. Light, editorial, warm.

## Palette direction

Anchored by deep pine green (`#2d4a3e`) as the brand color, used sparingly — primarily for buttons, accents, and the booking section. The primary surface is a crisp warm alpine white (`#fafaf7`), with a warm linen (`#f2ede2`) for alternating sections. The accent is a burnt amber (`#b86b2a`) for eyebrows, CTAs, and the booking button — warmth, fireplace, wood. The overall page is light and airy. Only the booking CTA section uses the deep pine green background for contrast.

## Typography direction

Fraunces is the heading typeface — a variable optical-size serif with an editorial, slightly rustic quality. Set at light weight (300) for large display. Warm and characterful without feeling dated. DM Sans is the body font — clean, neutral, modern at light-to-regular weight. The pairing reads as editorial: Fraunces for emotion, DM Sans for clarity.

## Decorative vocabulary

- Eyebrow text in uppercase amber with wide letter-spacing
- Short amber rule (`bb-divider`) under section headings
- Reveal-on-scroll animations (subtle fade + lift)
- Fixed translucent header that overlaps a full-viewport hero photo
- Photo callouts overlaid on lounge imagery with a left-side gradient

## Layout

Sections alternate between surface colors. Hero is full-viewport mountain photo with editorial text overlay. Room cards use a clean grid with hover states. Shuttle section is two-column with destination cards. Amenities uses a photo + icon grid. Booking section is centered and dark green. Generous whitespace throughout.
