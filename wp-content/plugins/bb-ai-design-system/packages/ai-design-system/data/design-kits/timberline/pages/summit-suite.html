The Room

Built around a long, slow morning.

The Summit Suite occupies the top-floor corner of the lodge — two walls of windows, the kind of light that makes you reconsider sleeping in. Wide-plank pine floors run warm under bare feet thanks to the heating beneath them. The bed is a king with a real wool topper and linens you'll want to write home about.

The bath is its own room: a deep cast-iron soaking tub set against the window, walk-in shower with rainfall head, and the kind of small details — heated towel rack, matte stone counters, a stocked espresso bar by the door — that turn a hotel stay into something closer to a friend's beautifully kept cabin.

The fireplace is real and wood-burning, with a stack of split pine resupplied each afternoon. And outside, on the private deck, two Adirondack chairs face the treeline and the slope of mountain beyond it. Coffee out there at sunrise is the whole point.
