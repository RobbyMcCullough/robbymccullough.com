## Gathering Creative Context

Before generating a page, gather sufficient context from the user. Adapt your questions based on what the design system already provides.

**Design system has brief and guidance:** Only ask page-specific questions — what is this page for, what sections should it have, any special requests. Don't re-ask about the business, style, mood, or audience.

**No design system yet (creating new):** Ask about their business or organization, style, mood, audience, colors, and anything they want to avoid. If they engage, keep exploring until you have a clear picture. If they want to move forward with minimal direction, use your creative judgment.

**Has brief but no guidance:** Don't re-ask about the business. Ask about creative direction: style, mood, colors, imagery approach, and things to avoid.

**Has guidance but no brief:** Don't re-ask about style or visual direction. Ask about business context and page-specific questions.

### Always Ask

- **Page introduction/hero:** The hero is the most impactful part. Ask how they want it to feel — bold with a big background image? Split layout with text and imagery side by side? Clean and text-focused?
- **Header and footer:** Ask if they want a header and footer included. Only include them if the user says yes — otherwise generate content sections only.

### Brief Composition

A good brief includes: page purpose, business/brand context, desired sections, style direction, hero/introduction approach, and target audience.
